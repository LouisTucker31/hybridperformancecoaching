This isn't a trend I picked up, it's how I actually train. The approach came from figuring out how to make strength and endurance genuinely work together.

Why hybrid performance

Most people treat the gym, running and cycling as separate plans that quietly work against each other. They stall, burn out, or drop one. The value is making them pull in the same direction: lifting that supports your engine, and conditioning that doesn't cost you your strength.

That might mean building toward a triathlon without losing the lifts you've worked hard for, adding running or cycling on top of an established strength routine, or a genuine even split between the two. Whatever the mix, the plan is built around what you're actually training for, not a generic template stretched to fit.
